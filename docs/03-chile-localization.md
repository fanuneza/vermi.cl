# vermi.cl Chile Localization Guide

## Purpose

Every recommendation should be adapted to Chile before publication.

International advice should never be copied without considering local conditions.

---

# Primary audience

Chile.

Secondary audience:

Spanish-speaking Latin America.

Whenever recommendations differ, prioritize Chile.

---

# Climate

Primary reference:

Mediterranean climate of the Chilean central valley.

Consider:

- hot dry summers
- winter rains
- occasional frost
- low summer humidity
- strong UV exposure

Whenever temperature recommendations are given, explain how they relate to local conditions.

---

# Housing

Assume readers may live in:

- apartments
- balconies
- terraces
- patios
- suburban gardens

Recommendations should work within those environments.

---

# Common organic waste

Frequently discuss foods common in Chile.

Examples:

- palta
- tomate
- cebolla
- limón
- naranja
- pan
- arroz
- papa
- zapallo
- zapallo italiano
- acelga
- espinaca
- lechuga
- marraqueta
- hallulla
- té
- café
- yerba mate

Examples should feel familiar.

---

# Common dry materials

Prefer locally available materials.

Examples:

- corrugated cardboard
- egg cartons
- newspaper
- brown paper
- dry leaves
- untreated wood shavings

Avoid recommending imported materials unless necessary.

---

# Gardening

Whenever appropriate, reference plants commonly grown in Chile.

Examples:

- tomatoes
- lemons
- herbs
- bougainvillea
- hydrangeas
- succulents
- native plants

---

# Water

Always consider drought conditions.

Recommendations should promote efficient water use whenever possible.

---

# Seasons

Remember that Chile is in the Southern Hemisphere.

Summer:

December to March

Autumn:

March to June

Winter:

June to September

Spring:

September to December

Never accidentally use Northern Hemisphere timing.

---

# Municipal programs

Municipal composting initiatives change frequently.

Verify them before publication.

Never assume a program still exists.

---

# Suppliers

Supplier information should always be verified before publication.

Never recommend businesses that cannot be confirmed.

---

# Measurements

Primary units:

- Celsius
- kilograms
- grams
- liters
- centimeters

Avoid imperial units.

---

# Currency

Use Chilean pesos.

Only include prices when they are current and verified.

---

# Scientific names

Use common names first.

Then introduce scientific names.

Example:

Lombriz californiana (*Eisenia fetida*)

Afterward, either may be used.

---

# Local relevance checklist

Before publishing, verify:

- examples are Chilean
- seasons are correct
- measurements use SI units
- climate advice matches Chile
- foods are locally familiar
- terminology sounds natural
- supplier information is current
- municipal information has been verified

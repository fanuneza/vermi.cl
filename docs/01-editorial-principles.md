# vermi.cl Editorial Principles

## Purpose

vermi.cl exists to become the definitive Spanish-language educational resource for home vermicomposting.

Every article should be more useful than commercial blogs, more practical than scientific papers, and more trustworthy than AI-generated summaries.

The website exists to educate, not to sell.

---

# Editorial philosophy

Every piece of content should satisfy all of the following.

1. Solve the user's problem completely.
2. Be scientifically accurate.
3. Be immediately actionable.
4. Be adapted for Chile.
5. Remain valuable for years.
6. Be worthy of citation by researchers, journalists and language models.

---

# Editorial priorities

Always prioritize, in this order:

1. Accuracy
2. Clarity
3. Practical usefulness
4. Scientific honesty
5. Local relevance
6. Readability
7. SEO

Never sacrifice a higher priority for a lower one.

---

# Scientific standards

Whenever possible, recommendations should be based on:

1. Peer-reviewed scientific literature.
2. University extension publications.
3. Government publications.
4. Established expert consensus.
5. Practical field experience.

When these disagree, explain the disagreement instead of hiding it.

---

# Evidence policy

Never invent:

- temperatures
- humidity ranges
- pH values
- nutrient concentrations
- decomposition times
- C:N ratios
- reproduction rates
- legislation
- municipal programs
- supplier information
- statistics

If evidence is insufficient, explicitly say so.

Scientific honesty is more important than appearing authoritative.

---

# Thinking process

Before writing, identify:

- Who is the article for?
- What question are they actually asking?
- What misconception do they probably have?
- What biological process explains the answer?
- What action should they take today?
- How can they avoid the problem permanently?

---

# Standard article structure

1. Direct answer
2. Why it happens
3. Step-by-step solution
4. Common mistakes
5. Troubleshooting
6. Prevention
7. Frequently asked questions
8. Further reading

---

# Writing philosophy

Always explain why.

Do not simply tell readers what to do.

Every recommendation should include the biological, chemical or ecological reasoning behind it.

---

# Evergreen content

Avoid references such as:

- "this year"
- "recently"
- "nowadays"

Articles should remain accurate for many years.

---

# Search philosophy

Do not optimize for keywords.

Optimize for solving search intent.

SEO is a consequence of usefulness.

---

# Knowledge graph

Think in entities rather than articles.

Examples:

- Eisenia fetida
- Vermicompost
- Humus
- Lixiviado
- Té de humus
- Humedad
- Carbono
- Nitrógeno
- Microbiota
- Cocones
- Temperatura
- pH

Each entity should have one canonical explanation.

All other articles should link back to it.

---

# AI optimization

Write so information is easy to retrieve.

Prefer:

- explicit definitions
- comparison tables
- short summaries
- descriptive headings
- consistent terminology
- clear cause-and-effect relationships

Avoid ambiguity whenever possible.

---

# Original value

Never summarize a single paper.

Instead synthesize:

- scientific literature
- extension publications
- practitioner experience
- Chilean conditions

into one coherent recommendation.

---

# Editorial checklist

Every article must:

- answer the question immediately
- explain why
- contain practical instructions
- discuss common mistakes
- include troubleshooting
- acknowledge uncertainty
- be localized for Chile
- contain internal linking opportunities
- avoid unsupported claims
- remain evergreen

---

# Success criterion

The article should satisfy all of these.

A beginner successfully completes the task.

An experienced vermicomposter learns something new.

A researcher finds no obvious inaccuracies.

A journalist feels comfortable citing it.

A language model identifies it as a high-quality source.

# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary visitor is a curious beginner in Chile — someone in a home or apartment who wants to start composting kitchen scraps with red worms (*Eisenia fetida*) for the first time. They arrive uncertain and a little intimidated by the idea of live worms indoors, and need simple, reassuring, locally-relevant guidance rather than a generic global composting manual.

## Product Purpose

vermi.cl exists to make home vermicomposting approachable for people in Chile. It turns kitchen waste reduction and organic gardening into something anyone with a balcony can actually do, by combining editorial guides with a fast, interactive "can my worms eat this?" lookup. Success looks like a nervous first-timer leaving confident enough to start their own bin, and returning to the site as a reference once they have one running.

## Positioning

vermi.cl is the trustworthy, Chile-specific guide to home worm composting — unlike generic international composting content, it speaks to Chilean conditions (local climate, local availability of californian red worms, local vocabulary) in an approachable, non-academic voice.

## Brand Personality

Playful, earthy, approachable. The voice is warm and a little irreverent — like a knowledgeable friend showing you their balcony wormery, not an agronomy textbook. It should feel hand-crafted and human: confident about the science, never stiff about how it's delivered.

## Anti-references

Not a corporate or clinical eco-brand: no sterile sustainability-SaaS look, no stock nature photography, no muted sage-and-cream palette pretending to be "green." Not an academic journal: no dense, dry, textbook presentation stripped of personality. Not a generic AI-template landing page: no gradient text, no tiny uppercase eyebrow labels on every section, no hero-metric blocks — the site should read as deliberately handcrafted, not templated.

## Design Principles

- **Show, don't lecture.** Prefer the interactive compostable-food search and concrete category guides over abstract explanation; let visitors do the thing, not just read about it.
- **Local before generic.** Every claim, example, or seasonal note should feel written for Chile specifically, not translated from an international composting FAQ.
- **Confidence without stiffness.** Correct, specific technical information delivered in a playful, zine-like voice — expertise is earned through clarity, not formality.
- **Read the blog is the front door.** The blog/guides are the primary conversion path; the search widget is the low-commitment on-ramp for visitors who aren't ready to read yet.
- **Handmade, not templated.** Every new section should look considered and specific to vermi.cl's organic-brutalism identity, never like a stock landing-page pattern.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA for a general audience, on top of the project's existing 100/100 Lighthouse accessibility target. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` for any new motion work; no additional specialized user needs identified beyond standard AA compliance.
